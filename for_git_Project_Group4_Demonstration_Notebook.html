Positional spending and Team Performance Analysis¶

Overall project objectives¶

The National Football League is a professional American football league consisting of 32 teams, divided equally between the National Football Conference and the American Football Conference. NFL Teams spend a lot of money on players in different positions on their roster and want their spending to translate into better performance for their teams therefore creating value for them. In order to understand how these additions will help them, the teams would benefit from an analysis to examine positional spending and its win percentage and then the ability to predict the estimated cap hit of the draft picks across all 32 teams in the NFL from 2013-2018.

At the outset, we are making three hypotheses, posing the following questions: -

  1. Does the overall spending (cap hit) of a team impact its win/loss percentage?
  2. What is the impact of spending on specific player position groups for the team? (Will my offense or defense define the win percentage during the year in terms of how much we need to spend on them?)
  3. If a player with certain demographics is drafted to a particular team, depending on his position how much is he likely to cost the team?

Dataset description¶

Method used to get data : Web Scraping.
Sites Scarped : Spotrac.com, ProFootballReference.com and ESPN.com

From Spotrac.com we have extracted data for each team basis the positions of each player, their age, the number of players in that position, the cap value in dollars and the as a percentage of total cap, and the ranking for that position in the NFL for each of the years from 2013-2018.

From Pro-football-reference, we have extracted data for all of these years for the same teams, with the number of games they have won, lost and tied, their win percentage, the points scored for and against, the average strength of opposition and their own offensive and defensive quality. Further, for each player in a team, their position, age, height, weight, college and experience i.e. their demographics has been extracted

From ESPN.com, we have extracted the win-loss-tie record for home and away for these teams, their record in the division and the conference and their end win/loss streak.

def readsite(site,year):
    #Read the site for AFC
    pro_2018_afc=pd.read_html(site)[0]
    #Drop rows which have totals
    pro_2018_afc=pro_2018_afc.drop([0,5,10,15],axis=0)
    #Drop columns with margin of victory and the simple rating system
    pro_2018_afc=pro_2018_afc.drop(['MoV','SRS'],axis=1)
    #Remove special characters
    pro_2018_afc.Tm=pro_2018_afc.Tm.str.rstrip('*')
    pro_2018_afc.Tm=pro_2018_afc.Tm.str.rstrip('+')
    #Add the year of the data
    pro_2018_afc['Year']=year
    #Same steps as above for NFC
    pro_2018_afc.Tm=pro_2018_afc.Tm.astype(str)
    pro_2018_nfc=pd.read_html(site)[1]
    pro_2018_nfc=pro_2018_nfc.drop([0,5,10,15],axis=0)

    pro_2018_nfc.Tm=pro_2018_nfc.Tm.str.rstrip('+')
    pro_2018_nfc['Year']=year
    pro_2018_nfc.Tm=pro_2018_nfc.Tm.astype(str)
    #Merge the two tables and drop the additonal column

#Define function teamlist to take the input of a list of sites for all years and the name of the team
def teamslist (linklist,team):
    #Intialize an empty DataFrame
    df=pd.DataFrame()
    #Initialize year as 2013 because we start scraping data from 2013
    year=2018
    #For loop to clean the data
    for link in linklist:
        #Read teh site link
        df1=pd.read_html(link)[0]
        #Keep only required columns
        df1=df1[['Player','Age','Wt','Ht','Yrs','College/Univ']]
        #Delete the total row
        df1=df1.drop(df1.tail(1).index,axis=0)
        #Remove special characters
        df1.Player=df1.Player.str.rstrip('*')
        #Convert height from feet to inches
        df1['ft']=df1.Ht.str.split('-').str[0]
        df1['point']=df1.Ht.str.split('-').str[1]
        df1['ft']=df1['ft'].astype(float)
        df1['point']=df1['point'].astype(float)
        df1.insert(4,'Ht (Inch)',df1.ft*12+df1.point)
        #Plot Year and Team Name
        df1['Year']=year
        df1['TeamShort']=team
        df1.drop(['Ht','ft','point'],axis=1,inplace=True)
        #Append the data to the data frame
        df=pd.concat([df,df1])
        df.reset_index(inplace=True)
        df=df.drop('index',axis=1)
        #Increase the year by 1
        year+=1
    return(df)

#Writing a function to clean all scraped data from 32 teams, for 6 years.
def teamspend (linklist,team):
    year=2018
    df = pd.DataFrame()
    #Run a for loop to clean all the data, eliminate irrelevant columns and merge the dataframes 
    for link in linklist:
        spend = pd.read_html(link)[0]
        spend.drop(columns=['Unnamed: 0','Unnamed: 1'],inplace=True)
        Name = []
        for i in spend['Player']:
            Name.append( ' '.join(i.split()[:2]))
        Position = []
        for j in spend['Player']:
            Position.append( ' '.join(j.split()[2:]))
        spend['Name'] = Name
        spend['Position'] = Position
        spend.drop(columns='Player',inplace=True)
        spend['TeamShort'] = team
        spend['Year'] = year
        spend = spend[['TeamShort','Name','Position','cap hit','Year']]
        df=pd.concat([df,spend])
        year+=1
    return(df)

Direction of Analysis¶

Since, our objective is to explain the impact of positional spending on the team's success, most of our analysis is centered around that aspect.

We analyse the different aspects of our dataset with respect to teams, their players and the cap hits these players have on the team's budget. We try to identify and analyse the impact of the absolute cap hit with the win loss ratio. We then go to analyse the impact of each of three major positional groups on the same win/loss ratio. We then try to identify each individual position impact on the team performance and if grouping them does give us better information.

We next understand the distribution of our spending. Using this information, we identify the appropriate regression to run. Review it against a standard type of regression and identify which would be the best for us to use to predict how much should we spend for a new draft.
